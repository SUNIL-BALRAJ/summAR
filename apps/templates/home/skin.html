Exercises

Yoga

Yoga is known for reducing stress and promoting relaxation, which can have positive effects on the skin by minimizing stress-related skin issues.

Pilates

Pilates focuses on core strength, flexibility, and body awareness, which can help improve circulation and skin health.

Cardiovascular Exercises

Engaging in moderate aerobic exercises like brisk walking, jogging, or cycling can promote blood circulation and help deliver essential nutrients to the skin.

Dancing

Dancing is a fun way to exercise and can improve blood flow, which is beneficial for the skin.

Calisthenics

Bodyweight exercises like push-ups, squats, and lunges can improve muscle tone and overall physical health, which may positively impact the skin.

Diet Plan

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Foods rich in omega-3 fatty acids, such as fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines) and chia seeds, can help reduce inflammation and keep the skin supple.

Colourful Vegetables and Fruits

Consume a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables like carrots, spinach, berries, and oranges, as they provide antioxidants that protect the skin from damage.

Probiotics

Probiotic-rich foods like yogurt and fermented foods (kimchi, sauerkraut) can support gut health, which may indirectly improve skin conditions.

Water

Staying well-hydrated with water and herbal teas can help keep the respiratory passages moist and aid in thinning mucus.
